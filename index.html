Project Type

MoneyHero

Timeline
June – July 2024

02 Problem

Problem

The Personal Loan Tactical Blog suffered from a severely low 3% "Apply" conversion rate and high page bounce rates, directly impacting affiliate revenue targets.

The page layout was cluttered with overlapping content and lacked a clear information hierarchy, making it difficult for different user types to navigate the page effectively.

Active buyers struggled to locate buried call-to-action (CTA) buttons, preventing them from quickly applying for financial products. At the same time, users seeking financial education felt overwhelmed by dense, unnavigable text, increasing the likelihood that they would leave the page early.

The business therefore required a scalable solution that could increase affiliate applications while strictly adhering to SEO structures, ensuring the blog continued to maintain its organic search dominance.

03 Research and Insights

User Journey Map: Savvy Shopper User

User Journey Map: Educational Advice seeker

Analytics Review:

Google Analytics showed massive user drop-offs immediately after the top hero banner, indicating a failure to capture initial user intent.

User Personas:

We identified a behavioral clash between two distinct groups: "Jetso Hunters" (active, high-intent loan applicants) and financial learners (low-intent researchers).

Navigation Friction:

The lack of internal jump links trapped users, preventing them from seamlessly transitioning between reading educational content and entering the application flow.

04 Design Strategy

Dual-Path UX

Separate user intents through distinct interaction models for readers versus buyers, ensuring neither persona feels alienated.

Mobile-First Scannability

Ensure long-form educational text is easily digestible on mobile screens to combat reading fatigue.

Technical Feasibility & Constraints

Execute all design decisions within HubSpot's strict HTML/CSS limitations to ensure frontend viability, proving an ability to design for real-world technical constraints.

05 Solution

Intent-Based Routing:

Two clear CTAs immediately separate users who want to learn from those ready to compare, streamlining their journey.

Adaptive Navigation:

Due to HubSpot coding limitations that prevented a global sticky menu, a collapsible Table of Contents was strategically placed at the bottom of each section. This allows users to easily reach and expand the menu as they finish reading a block of content, letting them jump between sections without endless vertical scrolling.

Progressive Disclosure:

Accordions are used to hide dense text, keeping the page clean and reducing cognitive load for mobile users.

Simplified Comparison:

Complex desktop tables are replaced with highly scannable rows that highlight only the top three decision-making metrics alongside prominent "Apply Now" buttons.

Overall Impact:

This design successfully navigates technical constraints while minimizing friction and user fatigue, making it effortless to digest complex financial data and convert quickly on a small screen.

06 Impact

[End Result] Successfully improved CTA click-through visibility for active buyers, driving a measurable uplift in top-of-funnel leads.

Significantly reduced page bounce rates for educational users by improving content digestion.

Shipped the fully optimized page within HubSpot's technical constraints, demonstrating strong cross-functional execution and hybrid design-engineering capabilities.
